# Drafting and Notation

Draft from verified evidence, not from plausible narrative. Use direct scientific prose and make uncertainty visible.

## Evidence-first drafting

For each paragraph:

1. State one purpose or claim.
2. Link it to a verified evidence-ledger entry or mark it unresolved.
3. Match the verb and certainty to the design and evidence: distinguish describes, associates, supports, suggests, and demonstrates.
4. State material conditions, uncertainty, alternatives, and limitations close to the claim.
5. Cite only verified sources that directly support the statement.

Never invent citations, values, quotations, analyses, results, or explanations. Replace unsupported specifics with an explicit marker such as `[evidence needed: effect estimate and interval]`, or narrow the sentence to what evidence supports.

## Notation registry

Maintain one canonical registry:

| Concept | Canonical term | Symbol | Units | First definition | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|

Define terms and symbols at first use. Use one symbol form for one concept and different symbols for different concepts. Preserve case, subscripts, superscripts, vectors, units, and sign conventions. Render equations in the target format: Markdown math where supported, or explicit portable notation otherwise.

Number only referenced equations, figures, tables, and sections. Resolve every cross-reference against an existing target after edits. Do not guess missing numbers; leave a conspicuous unresolved marker and report it.

Final pass: compare prose with the evidence ledger, scan terminology and symbols against the registry, and verify all citations and cross-references resolve.
