# Literature and Citations

Keep the research library separate from manuscript citations. Cite sources because they support a claim, not because they were collected.

## Literature workflow

1. Turn the question into concepts, synonyms, inclusion limits, and a reproducible search record: source searched, query, date, filters, and result count when available.
2. Prefer primary or authoritative sources appropriate to the claim. Record why each retained source matters.
3. Verify each source against the source itself or a reliable catalog. Capture authors, title, container, year, pages or article number, and identifiers only when present.
4. Deduplicate by verified identifier, then by normalized bibliographic identity. Do not merge merely similar works.
5. Maintain two sets:
   - **Working library:** relevant records retained for research, including uncited items.
   - **Cited set:** verified records that support claims in the current manuscript.
6. For quotations and specific claims, record an exact page, section, figure, table, paragraph, or other stable locator.
7. Before delivery, ensure every in-text citation resolves to one bibliography entry and every bibliography entry is cited. Uncited working-library items stay outside the manuscript bibliography.

## Verify sources

Never invent metadata, identifiers, quotations, locators, or source content. Flag incomplete or conflicting records. If search or source access is unavailable, state what could not be verified and provide the exact query, record, or document the user must retrieve. RIS and BibTeX are exchange formats, not proof that metadata is correct.
