# Research Planning

Plan before prose. Match the plan to the study rather than forcing quantitative or disciplinary assumptions.

## Build the plan

1. State the purpose, intended audience, document type, and decision the work should inform.
2. Define the contribution against what is already known. Mark unverified novelty claims as gaps for literature checking.
3. Write a focused research question. Add hypotheses only when the design uses them.
4. Classify the study provisionally: experimental, observational, clinical, qualitative, mixed-methods, computational, review/meta-analysis, theoretical, or other.
5. Arrange a claim sequence: context → gap → approach → supported findings or expected evidence → bounded implications.
6. Create an evidence map for every planned claim:

| Planned claim | Needed evidence | Available source/data | Gap or limitation |
|---|---|---|---|

7. List known gaps, dependencies, and decisions needed before drafting.

## Boundaries

- Do not invent findings, novelty, sources, data, or methods.
- Do not require numerical baselines, hypotheses, power calculations, or experiments unless applicable to the question and design.
- Send source retrieval and metadata to `literature-and-citations`.
- Send data provenance and claim-level evidence records to `data-and-evidence`.
- Send design-specific analytic checks to `study-design-and-reporting`.
- Stop when purpose, contribution, question, claim sequence, evidence map, and unresolved gaps are explicit enough to guide the next task.
