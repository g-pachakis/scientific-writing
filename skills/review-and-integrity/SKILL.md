# Review and Integrity

Scale review to risk and purpose. Review dimensions directly; do not impersonate named experts or require parallel agents.

## Select rigor

- **Quick:** bounded internal or local review; check scientific correctness, evidence alignment, clarity, and obvious integrity risks.
- **Standard:** full-document review; add methods/results coherence, citations, notation, visuals, reproducibility, and applicable disclosures.
- **Submission:** standard review plus venue requirements, complete declarations, generated-output inspection, and fresh final checks.

Sequential review is always valid. Report findings as:

| Severity | Location | Finding | Evidence | Required action |
|---|---|---|---|---|

`Blocking` means unsupported/fabricated content, material scientific error, unresolved ethics or consent need, integrity concern, missing critical evidence, or unmet required submission item. `Non-blocking` means optional clarity, style, or polish. Do not turn optional preferences into gates.

## Integrity dimensions

Check when applicable:

- approval, consent, participant privacy, animal welfare, safety, and legal restrictions;
- authorship eligibility and contribution statements such as CRediT;
- conflicts of interest, funding, sponsor role, and acknowledgments;
- citation, quotation, plagiarism, image, and data integrity;
- protocol, registration, deviations, selective reporting, and corrections;
- data, code, materials, and analysis availability statements;
- reproducibility of inputs, methods, environment, and outputs.

Never invent approvals, registrations, contributions, disclosures, availability, or completed checks. Sensitive unpublished data must not be delegated or exposed without authorization.

Before a pass claim, rerun applicable checks and cite fresh evidence. If tools or files are unavailable, mark the gate incomplete and specify the manual verification needed.
