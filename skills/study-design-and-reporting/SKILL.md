# Study Design and Reporting

Apply checks that fit the actual design. Do not impose one field's checklist on another.

## Match guidance to study type

Classify the work as experimental, observational, clinical, qualitative, mixed-methods, computational, review/meta-analysis, theoretical, or other. If classification changes what must be reported and cannot be inferred, ask.

For any study, verify:

- question or estimand matches design, sampling, measurements, and analysis;
- inclusion, exclusion, comparison, outcome, and stopping rules are explicit where applicable;
- assumptions are stated and checked or bounded;
- estimates include effect size and uncertainty when meaningful, not significance alone;
- missing data, multiplicity, and sample-size or power rationale are handled when applicable;
- validation, calibration, sensitivity, and robustness checks match the method;
- limitations distinguish design constraints from execution failures;
- materials, data, code, software versions, parameters, and computational environment are reported sufficiently for reproducibility when relevant.

For qualitative work, report recruitment, context, researcher role/reflexivity, analytic procedure, saturation or information-power rationale when used, and support interpretations with traceable material. For reviews, report search, screening, appraisal, synthesis, and heterogeneity choices. For simulations and computational studies, report test cases, reference truth or benchmark, error distribution and uncertainty, validation, sensitivity, seeds where relevant, and environment.

Select an applicable current external reporting guideline from study type and venue requirements. Check the current authoritative source; do not claim compliance from memory or embed an exhaustive copy. Record each applicable item as met, not met, or not applicable with evidence.

Never invent design details, statistical outputs, approvals, or compliance.
